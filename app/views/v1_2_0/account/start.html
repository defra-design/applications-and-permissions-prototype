Apply for a Bovine Tuberculosis (TB) movement licence

Movement types this service is for

You should only use this service if you are moving animals from one of these origins:

  • TB restricted farm
  • approved finishing unit (AFU), including enhanced with grazing (AFUE)

And they are going to one of these destinations:

  • slaughter
  • dedicated sale for TB (orange market)
  • approved finishing unit (AFU), including enhanced with grazing (AFUE)

If you are moving animals from a TB restricted farm to slaughter, you do not need to use this service to apply for a licence.

You should have a general licence in the information pack sent to you from the Animal and Plant Health Agency (APHA). The pack was posted to you when your herd was placed under TB restriction.

For movements onto your farm or premises, complete the TB204 form.

Before you start

Licences cannot be assessed or issued if:

  • reactors are still on your farm or premises
  • cattle are awaiting skin or blood test results

Contact your vet if you are unsure whether the tests have been sent. If APHA has not received the tests, there could be delays in processing your licence.

To apply for a licence, you need the following information:

  • name of the County Parish Holding (CPH) owner
  • County Parish Holding (CPH) number and address where the animals are moving from
  • where the animals are moving to, for example the type of market
  • email address to send the licence to

It can take up to 5 working days to process your application. It may take longer if veterinary checks are required, or we need further information.

Get help

If you need help using the service you can call 03000 200 301 and choose the option for TB licencing. Lines are open from Monday to Friday between 8.30am and 5pm.
